Golden Sweetness in Every Bite

Rainier cherries are prized for their golden‑yellow skin with a blush of red and their incredibly delicate sweetness.

About Rainier

Named after Mount Rainier, this variety was developed in 1952 by crossing Bing and Van cherries at Washington State University.

Flavor & Use

With higher sugar content than most sweets, Rainiers shine when eaten fresh—handle gently, as their tender skin bruises easily.

Quick Facts

  • 🎨 Distinctive golden‑yellow with red blush
  • 🍯 Exceptionally high Brix (sugar)
  • 🕒 Short season: late June – early July
  • ⛰️ Named after Washington’s tallest volcano
